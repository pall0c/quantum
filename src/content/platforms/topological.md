Majorana fermions in nanowires and anyons in fractional quantum Hall systems

<br />

<trapping-title>Trapping Technique</trapping-title>

1. <trapping-notes>Majorana zero modes localized at defects in superconducting-semiconductor nanowires</trapping-notes>
1. <trapping-notes>Topological phase requires specific material properties and magnetic field</trapping-notes>
1. <trapping-notes>Majorana modes appear at wire ends or domain walls between phases</trapping-notes>
1. <trapping-notes>Anyons emerge in fractional quantum Hall systems at specific filling factors</trapping-notes>
1. <trapping-notes>Anyons are quasiparticle excitations in 2D electron gas</trapping-notes>
1. <trapping-notes>Strong magnetic fields and low temperatures required for topological phases</trapping-notes>

<br />

<gate-title>Gate Mechanism</gate-title>

1. <gate-notes>Braiding operations: physically moving Majorana modes around each other</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>Braiding creates non-Abelian phase factors, implementing quantum gates</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>Topological protection: gates are fault-tolerant by design</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>Measurement-based gates: measure topological charge to perform operations</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>Fusion rules: combining anyons creates entanglement</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>No local perturbations can affect topological qubits during braiding</gate-notes>
1. <gate-notes>Gate operations depend only on topology of braid, not precise path</gate-notes>
