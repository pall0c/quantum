title: Theory
---

Foundations:

1. Energy quantization: Max Planck (1900) - Blackbody radiation
1. Light quanta: Albert Einstein (1905) - Photoelectric effect; wave–particle duality
1. Quantized atomic orbits: Niels Bohr (1913) - Old quantum theory
1. Matter waves: Louis de Broglie (1924) - Wave–particle duality for particles

---

Core quantum mechanics:

1. Matrix mechanics: Werner Heisenberg (1925) - Observable-first formalism
1. Exclusion principle: Wolfgang Pauli (1925)
1. Wave mechanics: Erwin Schrödinger (1926) - Schrödinger equation
1. Probabilistic interpretation: Max Born (1926) - Wavefunction squared as probability
1. Uncertainty principle: Werner Heisenberg (1927)
1. General quantum formalism: Paul Dirac (1927) - Bra–ket notation
1. Hilbert space formulation: John von Neumann (1932) - Measurement theory

---

Interpretations:

1. Copenhagen interpretation: Niels Bohr; Werner Heisenberg (1920s–1930s) - Family of views
1. Many-worlds interpretation: Hugh Everett (1957)
1. Pilot-wave theory: Louis de Broglie; David Bohm (1927; 1952) - Deterministic hidden variables
1. Objective collapse theories: Ghirardi; Rimini; Weber; Roger Penrose (1980s–present)

---

Relativistic quantum theory & Quantum Field Theory:

1. Relativistic electron equation: Paul Dirac (1928) - Predicts antimatter
1. Quantum electrodynamics: Tomonaga; Schwinger; Feynman; Dyson (1940s) - Relativistic QFT of electromagnetism
1. Renormalization: Freeman Dyson; Gell-Mann; Low (1949–1954) - Renormalization group

---

Many-body and condensed matter:

1. Fermi liquid theory: Lev Landau (1930s–1950s) - Superfluidity
1. Bethe ansatz: Hans Bethe (1931) - Exact solutions in many-body systems
1. BCS theory: Bardeen; Cooper; Schrieffer (1957) - Superconductivity

---

The Standard Model:

1. Quark model: Murray Gell-Mann; George Zweig (1964)
1. Higgs mechanism: Peter Higgs; François Englert; Robert Brout (1964) - Mass generation
1. Electroweak theory: Weinberg; Salam; Glashow (1967–1968)
1. Quantum chromodynamics: Gross; Wilczek; Politzer (1970s) - Strong interaction
1. Standard Model: Particle physics community (1970s) - Unified gauge theory

---

Quantum gravity:

1. Quantum field theory in curved spacetime: Stephen Hawking (1970s) - Black hole radiation
1. Canonical quantum gravity: John Wheeler; Bryce DeWitt (1960s–1970s) - Wheeler–DeWitt equation
1. Loop quantum gravity: Carlo Rovelli; Lee Smolin (1990s)
1. String theory: Multiple authors (1960s–present) - Origin in hadron physics

---

Holography and dualities:

1. Holographic principle: Gerard ’t Hooft; Leonard Susskind (1993–1995)
1. AdS/CFT correspondence: Juan Maldacena (1997) - Gauge gravity duality

---

Modern quantum science:

1. Quantum information theory: Multiple authors (1980s–present) - Entanglement as a resource
1. Quantum computation: Richard Feynman; David Deutsch (1982–1996) - Computational model
1. Quantum algorithms: Peter Shor; Lov Grover (1994–1996)
1. Topological phases of matter: Robert Laughlin; Xiao-Gang Wen (1980s–1990s)
1. Quantum Hall effects: Multiple authors (1980s–present)
Anyons
1. Quantum simulation: Multiple authors (1990s–present) - Cold atoms; trapped ions
1. Quantum cryptography: Charles Bennett; Gilles Brassard (1984)

---

Quantum cosmology:

1. Wheeler–DeWitt equation: John Wheeler; Bryce DeWitt (1967)
1. Hartle–Hawking state: James Hartle; Stephen Hawking (1983) - No boundary proposal
