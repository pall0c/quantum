---
title: Fermions & Bosons
---
Nature is made of quantum fields
<br />
Fermions and bosons are two classes of excitations of those fields

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Fermions</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Matter particles</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Have half-integer spin (1/2, 3/2, ...)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Include electrons and quarks</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Responsible for the stability of matter</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Obey the Pauli exclusion principle</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Bosons</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Force carrying particles</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Have integer spin (0, 1, 2, ...)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Include photons, gluons, W and Z bosons and Higgs boson</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Responsible for the fundamental interactions between particles</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Do not obey the Pauli exclusion principle</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Fermion-Boson Interaction</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Fermions interact by exchanging bosons</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Exchanges are often described using virtual particles in quantum field theory</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>All observed forces (except gravity - pending) arise from these exchanges</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>The behavior of matter and forces in the universe emerges from this interaction</fermion-boson-notes>
