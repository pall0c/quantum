---
title: Hund's Rule
---

Hund's rule is an empirical rule that determines the ground state electron configuration of atoms and molecules, specifying how electrons fill degenerate orbitals.

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Three Rules</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Maximum multiplicity: Electrons fill orbitals to maximize total spin</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Maximum orbital angular momentum: For a given multiplicity, choose the state with maximum L</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Minimum total angular momentum: For atoms with less than half-filled shells, J = |L - S|; for more than half-filled, J = L + S</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>First Rule (Most Common)</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>When filling degenerate orbitals, electrons occupy separate orbitals with parallel spins</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Electrons pair up only after all degenerate orbitals contain one electron</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>This minimizes electron-electron repulsion by maximizing spatial separation</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Formulated by Friedrich Hund in 1925</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Physical Basis</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Electrons with parallel spins avoid each other due to exchange interaction</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Reduces Coulomb repulsion between electrons in degenerate orbitals</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Results in lower energy configuration compared to paired electrons</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Examples</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Carbon: 1s² 2s² 2p² - two unpaired electrons in different p orbitals</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Nitrogen: 1s² 2s² 2p³ - three unpaired electrons, one in each p orbital</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Oxygen: 1s² 2s² 2p⁴ - two unpaired electrons (one orbital has paired electrons)</fermion-boson-notes>
