---
title: Madelung Rule
---

The Madelung rule (also known as the n+l rule or the diagonal rule) is an empirical rule that describes the order in which atomic orbitals are filled with electrons in the ground state configuration of atoms.

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Key Principle</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Electrons fill orbitals in order of increasing n+l, where n is the principal quantum number and l is the azimuthal quantum number</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>When two orbitals have the same n+l value, the orbital with the lower n is filled first</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Named after Erwin Madelung, though the rule was actually formulated by others</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Provides a systematic way to determine electron configuration without solving the Schr√∂dinger equation</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Orbital Filling Order</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>1s (n+l = 1+0 = 1)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>2s (n+l = 2+0 = 2)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>2p (n+l = 2+1 = 3)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>3s (n+l = 3+0 = 3) - filled after 2p due to lower n</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>3p (n+l = 3+1 = 4)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>4s (n+l = 4+0 = 4) - filled before 3d due to lower n</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>3d (n+l = 3+2 = 5)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>4p (n+l = 4+1 = 5) - filled after 3d due to lower n</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>5s (n+l = 5+0 = 5) - filled after 4p due to lower n</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>4d, 5p, 6s, 4f, 5d, 6p, 7s, 5f, 6d, 7p (continuing pattern)</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Physical Basis</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>The rule reflects the balance between orbital energy and electron-electron interactions</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Lower n+l values generally correspond to lower energy orbitals</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Explains why 4s fills before 3d despite 3d having a lower principal quantum number</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Accounts for the structure of the periodic table and transition metal configurations</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Exceptions</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Some transition metals have exceptions (e.g., Cr, Cu) due to half-filled or fully-filled subshell stability</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Lanthanides and actinides show deviations due to f-orbital effects</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Heavier elements may not strictly follow the rule due to relativistic effects</fermion-boson-notes>
