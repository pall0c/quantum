---
title: Pauli Exclusion Principle
---

The Pauli exclusion principle is a fundamental quantum mechanical principle that states no two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously.

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Key Points</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Applies only to fermions (particles with half-integer spin)</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Two fermions cannot have the same set of quantum numbers</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Formulated by Wolfgang Pauli in 1925</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Explains the structure of the periodic table and electron shells</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Prevents matter from collapsing into a single quantum state</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Physical Consequences</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>Electrons in atoms occupy different orbitals, leading to atomic structure</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Creates degeneracy pressure in white dwarfs and neutron stars</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Underpins the stability of matter at the atomic level</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Essential for understanding chemical bonding and molecular structure</fermion-boson-notes>

<br />

<fermion-boson-title>Mathematical Formulation</fermion-boson-title>

1. <fermion-boson-notes>The wavefunction of identical fermions must be antisymmetric</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>Swapping two fermions changes the sign of the wavefunction</fermion-boson-notes>
1. <fermion-boson-notes>This antisymmetry ensures no two fermions can be in the same state</fermion-boson-notes>
